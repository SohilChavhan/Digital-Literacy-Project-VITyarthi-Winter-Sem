Do's for College Students:

Do audit your privacy settings regularly to control who can see your personal posts and location data.

Do use platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase your academic projects, code, and technical skills to future recruiters.

Do think before you post; assume everything you put online is permanent and searchable by future employers.

Do engage positively with peers and professors by sharing helpful resources and constructive comments.

Do keep your profile pictures and bios professional on networks associated with your career or university.

Don'ts for College Students:

Don't post emotional rants or negative comments about your university, professors, or peers.

Don't overshare sensitive personal information like your exact real-time location, phone number, or student ID card.

Don't engage in cyberbullying or feed internet trolls; block and report abusive behavior instead.

Don't use offensive language or share inappropriate media that could jeopardize future employment or academic standing.

Don't plagiarize or claim someone else's digital work, code, or art as your own without proper credit.
